Perito Moreno Glacier: the eighth wonder of the world


The Perito Moreno is one of the many glaciers that form the Los Glaciares National Park. All form the Patagonian Continental Ice (17,000-kilometers long) which is one of the most drinking water supplies in the world. Is that 90% of the world's freshwater is found in glacial areas and after Antarctica and Greenland, is this region of Argentina.

Waterfall

This was one of most powerful waterfalls in the district of Hope. Sending glacial melt-waters over its 44m precipice unchecked at a flow of around 500 cubic meters per second, we literally felt the ground tremble beneath our feet as we stood and watched such awesome power from up close! Positioned at the head of the impressive Jökulsárgljúfur (the Icelandic version of the Grand Canyon) while surrounded by three other major waterfalls on the same river, it epitomized the wild and raw landscape that characterized Iceland. Indeed, nature lovers from both near and far loved this waterfall.
